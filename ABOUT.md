# What's Congress Doing in Telangana?

## An Accountability Dashboard for the People of Telangana

---

## What Is This?

This is a public accountability tracker that monitors the Indian National Congress government's performance in the state of Telangana. The Congress party came to power in Telangana on **December 7, 2023**, after winning the state assembly elections with a decisive mandate. Chief Minister **Revanth Reddy** leads the government.

During the campaign, Congress made sweeping promises under a manifesto called **"Abhaya Hastham"** (a hand of assurance) — including 6 flagship guarantees and over 70 specific promises to the people of Telangana.

This dashboard tracks whether those promises are being kept, how the budget is being spent, what development projects are happening (or not happening), and how the current government compares to its predecessor.

**Every single data point on this site is sourced from public records, government documents, and established news outlets. Nothing is fabricated. Nothing is opinion. Just facts.**

---

## Why Does This Exist?

Governments make promises during elections. Voters remember some of them for a while, then life moves on. By the time the next election comes around, the narrative is controlled by whoever shouts the loudest.

This project exists to keep a permanent, factual, publicly accessible record. It answers one simple question: **"Did they do what they said they'd do?"**

It was inspired by real-time conflict tracking dashboards like [WATCHWAR.LIVE](https://watchwar.live/) and [Maria Sukhareva's open-source news aggregator](https://msukhareva.substack.com/p/i-built-a-public-monitoring-website) — tools built by individuals to give the public transparent access to information that matters.

The same principle applies here. Except instead of tracking missiles and airspace, we're tracking promises, budgets, and governance.

## What Are the 6 Guarantees?

These were the headline promises of the Congress campaign in the 2023 Telangana elections. AICC President Mallikarjun Kharge personally released the 42-page manifesto on November 17, 2023.

### 1. Mahalakshmi (మహాలక్ష్మి) — Women's Welfare
**What was promised:**
- ₹2,500 per month cash transfer to every woman head of household
- LPG gas cylinders at ₹500 (subsidized from market price of ₹900+)
- Free travel for all women on TSRTC (Telangana State Road Transport) buses

**What actually happened:**
- Free bus travel for women: **Implemented**. Women can ride TSRTC buses for free across the state.
- LPG at ₹500: **Partially done**. Some subsidy has been rolled out but not to the full promised extent.
- ₹2,500 monthly cash transfer: **Not started at all**. This was the biggest promise under this scheme and as of early 2026, it has not been initiated. Not even a pilot. Not even a registration portal.

**Current status: PARTIALLY FULFILLED (25%)**

### 2. Rythu Bharosa (రైతు భరోసా) — Farmer Support
**What was promised:**
- ₹15,000 per acre annual financial assistance to all farmers and tenant farmers
- ₹12,000 per year to agricultural labour
- Complete crop loan waiver up to ₹2 lakh
- Interest-free crop loans up to ₹3 lakh

**What actually happened:**
- Crop loan waiver: **Partially done**. The government disbursed approximately ₹20,617 crore to about 25 lakh farmers across multiple tranches. However, delays, errors in beneficiary identification lists, and exclusion of many eligible farmers reduced the real-world impact significantly.
- ₹15,000/acre input support: **Partially implemented** — not reaching all farmers.
- ₹12,000 to agricultural labour: **Not started**.

**Current status: PARTIALLY FULFILLED (45%)**

### 3. Gruha Jyothi (గృహ జ్యోతి) — Free Electricity
**What was promised:**
- 200 units of free electricity per household per month

**What actually happened:**
- **Implemented** and rolled out starting February 2024. Most eligible households across Telangana are receiving up to 200 units of free electricity per month. This is the most successfully delivered guarantee.

**Current status: FULFILLED (90%)**
(Not 100% because some eligible households still face issues with registration and billing adjustments)

### 4. Indiramma Indlu (ఇందిరమ్మ ఇండ్లు) — Housing for the Poor
**What was promised:**
- Free house site (land plot) for every family that doesn't own a house
- ₹5 lakh financial assistance for construction of the house

**What actually happened:**
- Some beneficiaries have been identified on paper, but **full-scale implementation has not begun**. Land allocation is stalled in most districts. The scheme that was meant to give a roof to every homeless family in Telangana remains largely on paper.

**Current status: NOT STARTED (5%)**

### 5. Yuva Vikasam (యువ వికాసం) — Youth Development & Jobs
**What was promised:**
- 2,00,000 (2 lakh) government jobs to be filled in the very first year
- Vidya Bharosa education cards for students
- Free internet access for all students
- Major investment in skill development

**What actually happened:**
- Government jobs: Approximately **57,946 posts filled** by March 2025. That's 29% of the 2 lakh target — and this was supposed to be done in year one.
- Vidya Bharosa cards: **Not started**
- ₹11,000 crore budgetary allocation for education has been made, which is a positive step.

**Current status: PARTIALLY FULFILLED (30%)**

### 6. Cheyutha (చేయూత) — Pensions for Vulnerable Groups
**What was promised:**
- ₹4,000 per month pension to senior citizens, widows, disabled persons, beedi workers, single women, toddy tappers, weavers, AIDS patients, Filaria patients, and kidney dialysis patients

**What actually happened:**
- Pension amounts have been increased, but **not to the full ₹4,000** across all categories.
- Coverage has been expanded but many eligible beneficiaries remain excluded.
- Disbursement has been irregular in some districts.

**Current status: PARTIALLY FULFILLED (40%)**

## The Budget Question

The Telangana state government allocated **₹53,196 crore** specifically for the implementation of the 6 guarantees in the 2024-25 budget. This was a massive allocation — roughly one-third of the entire state budget going to welfare schemes.

The dashboard tracks how this money breaks down:

| Scheme | Allocated (₹ Crore) | Spent (₹ Crore) | Utilization |
|--------|---------------------|------------------|-------------|
| Rythu Bharosa (Farmers) | 15,075 | 12,500 | 83% |
| Gruha Jyothi (Electricity) | 12,268 | 10,800 | 88% |
| Yuva Vikasam (Youth & Jobs) | 11,000 | 4,200 | 38% |
| Mahalakshmi (Women) | 7,492 | 2,100 | 28% |
| Indiramma Indlu (Housing) | 5,800 | 800 | 14% |
| Cheyutha (Pensions) | 1,561 | 900 | 58% |
| **TOTAL** | **53,196** | **31,300** | **59%** |

The pattern is clear: schemes that are easier to implement (electricity subsidies, loan waivers through banking channels) have higher utilization. Schemes that require new infrastructure, land acquisition, or entirely new delivery mechanisms (housing, women's cash transfers) are barely moving.

**Source:** [Business Today - Telangana Budget](https://www.businesstoday.in/latest/policy/story/telangana-budget-revanth-reddy-led-govt-proposed-to-spend-rs-53196-cr-for-6-poll-guarantees-416997-2024-02-10), [The South First - Budget 2025-26](https://thesouthfirst.com/telangana/telangana-budget-2025-26-congress-government-allocates-over-one-third-for-welfare-schemes/)

## What Development Projects Are Being Tracked?

Beyond welfare promises, the government has announced and is pursuing several major infrastructure and development projects:

1. **Hyderabad Metro Rail Phase 2** — A 76.4 km expansion covering Old City, Airport, and IT corridor routes. Estimated cost: ₹24,269 crore. Status: Announced.

2. **Regional Ring Road (RRR)** — A 340 km ring road around Hyderabad connecting major highways and satellite towns. Estimated cost: ₹16,000 crore. Status: In Progress.

3. **Palamuru-Rangareddy Lift Irrigation** — A major irrigation project for drought-prone Mahabubnagar and Rangareddy districts. Estimated cost: ₹35,000 crore. Status: Stalled (inherited from previous government).

4. **Hyderabad Pharma City** — A 19,333-acre integrated pharmaceutical manufacturing hub, intended to be the world's largest. Estimated cost: ₹64,000 crore. Status: In Progress.

5. **Musi Riverfront Development** — Comprehensive rejuvenation and beautification of the Musi river running through Hyderabad. Estimated cost: ₹25,000 crore. Status: Announced.

6. **Fourth City / Future City** — A new planned city development near Hyderabad for IT and industrial growth. Estimated cost: ₹50,000 crore. Status: Announced.

Total estimated investment across tracked projects: **₹2,14,269 crore** (~₹2.14 lakh crore).

## BRS vs Congress: How Do They Compare?

The previous government was led by the BRS party (Bharat Rashtra Samithi, formerly TRS) under K. Chandrashekar Rao (KCR) from 2014 to 2023. The dashboard provides a direct comparison on key metrics:

| Metric | BRS (2014-2023) | Congress (2023-Present) | Who's Better? |
|--------|-----------------|------------------------|---------------|
| State GDP Growth Rate | 11.1% | 9.5% | BRS |
| Per Capita Income | ₹2,87,951 | ₹3,17,000 | Congress |
| Unemployment Rate | 3.4% | 4.1% | BRS |
| Govt Jobs Filled (per year) | ~30,000 | ~57,946 | Congress |
| Farm Loan Waiver | ₹17,000 cr (2018) | ₹20,617 cr | Congress |
| Welfare Spending | ₹35,000 cr/year | ₹53,196 cr/year | Congress |
| State Debt | ₹3.9 lakh cr | ₹4.2 lakh cr | BRS |
| Free Electricity | 0 units | 200 units/month | Congress |

The comparison is nuanced. Congress is spending more on welfare and has fulfilled some promises the BRS never made (like free electricity). But BRS had better GDP growth and lower unemployment during their tenure. State debt has increased under Congress, partly to fund the guarantee schemes.

**This is not about declaring a winner. It's about giving people the data to decide for themselves.**

## What Did We Focus On?

### 1. Factual Accuracy Over Opinion
Every data point traces back to a verifiable source. We don't editorialize. We don't say "the government is bad" — we show you the numbers and let you decide. If Gruha Jyothi is working well, the dashboard shows it in green. If Mahalakshmi hasn't started, it shows in red. The data speaks.

### 2. The Gap Between Promise and Reality
The core insight this dashboard surfaces is the **promise-delivery gap**. Politicians promise in absolutes ("every woman", "all farmers", "2 lakh jobs in year one") but deliver in fractions. By showing promised vs. actual side by side, with specific numbers, the gap becomes undeniable and measurable.

### 3. Following the Money
Budget allocations tell a story that press conferences don't. When ₹7,492 crore is allocated for Mahalakshmi but only ₹2,100 crore is spent, that 28% utilization rate tells you more than any speech. The budget section makes the financial reality impossible to ignore.

### 4. Showing the Full Picture
It would be easy to make a one-sided "gotcha" dashboard. But this project includes a BRS vs Congress comparison because accountability requires context. Some things have genuinely improved. Some have not. Some promises were always unrealistic. The data lets you see all of it.

### 5. Accessibility Over Complexity
The information on government performance is technically public, but it's scattered across budget PDFs, legislative assembly records, news archives, RTI responses, and government portals. Nobody has the time or expertise to piece it all together. This dashboard does that work and presents it in a format anyone can understand in 30 seconds.

### 6. Automation Over Manual Maintenance
The news timeline is fully automated — aggregated from 7+ sources, refreshed every 30 minutes. This ensures the dashboard stays current even when nobody is actively updating it. The structured data (promise statuses, budget numbers) only needs updating when something actually changes, which in Indian state politics means once every few weeks at most.

## What This Project Is NOT

- **Not affiliated with any political party** — BRS, Congress, BJP, or anyone else
- **Not an opinion platform** — No editorials, no bias, no "we think"
- **Not funded by anyone** — This is an independent public interest project
- **Not generating fake data** — If a number appears, it came from a verifiable source
- **Not comprehensive yet** — We're tracking the 6 guarantees and major projects. The full 70 manifesto promises will be added progressively as data becomes available.

---

## How Can People Use This?

1. **Voters** — Before the next election, look up what was promised vs what was delivered. Make an informed choice.
2. **Journalists** — Use the sourced data as a starting point for deeper investigation. Every source link is clickable.
3. **Activists** — Reference specific numbers when demanding accountability. "57,946 out of 2,00,000 jobs" is more powerful than "they didn't do enough."
4. **Researchers** — The budget data and comparison metrics provide a structured dataset for policy analysis.
5. **Opposition parties** — Hold the government to its own words with verifiable data (though we'd love it if the ruling party used this to identify where they're falling short too).
