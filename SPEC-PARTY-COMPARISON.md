# Party Comparison — v1 Spec

Status: **PLANNED — not yet started. Build after district map ships.**

---

## The idea

A dedicated page (nav: "PARTIES" or "2028") that gives voters a clear, side-by-side view of where each major party actually stands — based on what they're *doing*, not just what they're saying.

Three parties: **Congress** (ruling), **BRS** (opposition), **BJP** (opposition).

---

## Why this matters

Most political coverage is reactive — "BJP protests X", "BRS raises Y in assembly." Nobody synthesizes it into a coherent worldview per party. With elections likely in 2028–2029, voters deserve a clear picture of each party's priorities and vision *before* the campaign noise hits.

---

## Format: Static editorial (v1)

Start with a written, well-researched comparison page — not a live dashboard. The data isn't dense enough yet to make a dynamic comparison credible or fair.

Think of it as a **living document**: written once, updated every few months as things develop.

---

## Structure (three-column layout)

Each party gets:
- **Current priorities** — what they're actually spending time on, based on actions
- **Key issues they're pushing** — in assembly, in protests, in media
- **Who they're targeting** — farmers, urban voters, minorities, IT workers, etc.
- **Their development vision** — where they'd spend, what they'd prioritize if in power
- **What they're opposing** — tells you as much as what they support

---

## Neutrality rules

This page is the highest-risk for credibility. Strict rules:
- Every claim must have a source (news article, assembly record, budget document)
- No editorializing — just "they did X" not "they did X which is bad"
- All three parties get equal section length and structure
- Update cadence must be consistent — can't update one party and leave others stale

## Non-goals for v1
- Real-time updates or data ingestion
- Crowdsourced contributions to party profiles
- Sentiment scoring or "winner" declarations
- Historical comparison pre-2023
